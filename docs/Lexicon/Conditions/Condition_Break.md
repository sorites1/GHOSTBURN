# Break

**Cause:** When a a person or situation pushes you to your limits, you \[Break\] and can no longer control your actions.

**Effect:** You attack, flee, or break down on the spot. If you attack, you target what you perceive to be the source of your ire, which could be a person, place, or thing. If you flee, you attempt to escape that perceived source. If you break down, you fall [[Prone](../../Lexicon/Conditions/Condition_Prone.md)] and pour out your emotions. If a [[Group](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Group.md)] is caused to \[Break\], individuals from the \[Group\] represent all three reactions. If a player caused the target to \[Break\], the target's \[Attitude\] towards that player drops to [[Hostile](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Hostile.md)]. If the target is [[Compliant](../../Lexicon/Conditions/Condition_Compliant.md)] when they \[Break\], they lose \[Compliant\].

**Removal:** At the start of your turn, make a [[Basic](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Basic.md)] Resilience roll with a DL of Easy (8). On your turn, you may spend 1 AP to make this \[Basic\] Resilience roll. For NPCs, the GM should make a \[Basic\] roll without a bonus (do not add Threat). Success means you regain control and lose \[Break\].
