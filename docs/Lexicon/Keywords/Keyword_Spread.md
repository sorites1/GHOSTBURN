# Spread

When you make an [[Attack](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Attack.md)] with a shotgun, it uses \[Spread\] to hit multiple targets that are [[Close](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Close.md)] to one another. To determine which targets are hit, identify the primary target and look for additional targets that are \[Close\] to them. \[Spread\] affects up to two additional targets (three total)]. The primary target takes damage according to the outcome of the \[Attack\] roll, and additional targets take [[Reduced](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Reduced.md)] damage. A weapon with \[Spread\] cannot be used to \[Attack\] a target that is [[Far](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Far.md)] away.

For example, you use a Graham H6 shotgun to make an \[Attack\] against a target. There are two additional targets standing \[Close\] to the first (primary) target. You make your Firearms roll to use [Single Shot](../../Skills_and_Tricks/Firearms/Single_Shot.md) and score a Success, which deals [[Normal Damage](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Normal_Damage.md)]. The H6's [[Damage Array](../../Playing_the_Game/Rolling_the_Dice/Damage_Array.md)] for Single Shot is 9/18/45, so the \[Normal Damage\] is 18. The primary target takes 18 points of damage. The two additional targets take damage from the next step down on the \[Damage Array\], which is \[Minimum Damage\]. The H6's \[Minimum Damage\] is 9, so the two additional targets each take 9 points of damage.
