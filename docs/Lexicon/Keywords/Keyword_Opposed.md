# Opposed

This trick uses an \[Opposed\] roll to determine the outcome instead of the standard resolution process. 

The **target** of the trick rolls first.

* If the target is an NPC, the GM rolls 2d10 and adds the NPC's [[Threat](../../Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Threat.md)] bonus. 
* If the target is a player-character, the player rolls 2d10 and adds their bonuses.

The target's roll sets the Difficulty Level for the trick. Match the roll to the [Difficulty Level](../../Playing_the_Game/Rolling_the_Dice/Difficulty_Level.md) chart to determine the DL to use to for the trick. For example, if the target rolls a 15, that translates to Moderate (14) DL because its range is 14 to 19. 

| Difficulty Level | DL Range |
| :--------------- | :------- |
| Extreme (32)     | 32 - 37  |
| Severe (26)      | 26 - 31  |
| Hard (20)        | 20 - 25  |
| Moderate (14)    | 14 - 19  |
| Easy (8)         | 8 - 13   |

Once the target's roll has determined the Difficulty Level of the trick, the character who initiated the trick rolls and resolves it as normal.
