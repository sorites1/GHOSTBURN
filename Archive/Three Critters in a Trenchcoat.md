**Three Critters in a Trenchcoat** is a silly, GM-less game for three players. 

First, the players must agree on what kind of critter to be. All players must be the same type of critter. If you prefer, you may determine this randomly by rolling on the table below. 


| 1d6 | Critter |
| --- | ------- |
| 1   | Goats   |
| 2   | Monkeys |
| 3   | Rats    |
| 4   | Dogs    |
| 5   | Pigs    |
| 6   | Wolves  |

Next, decide who will take what position in the trenchcoat. There are three positions: Feet, Hands, and Head.


| Position | Role                                                                                                                          |
| -------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Feet     | The Feet are responsible for deciding where to go. They can also kick and dance.                                              |
| Hands    | The Hands are responsible for interacting with things. They can also punch.                                                   |
| Head     | The Head is responsible for seeing things and talking. It can also bite and spit. The Head also wears a Human Mask and a Hat. |

As critters, you are test subjects in a secret science lab. As a result of the experiments performed on you, you are now super-intelligent and can speak. Together, you have also recently mastered Trenchcoat technology. By stacking yourselves three critters tall, donning a trenchcoat, mask and hat, you can pass yourselves off as a human. 

The first thing you need to do is figure out how to escape the lab where you are being held. You successfully overpowered one of the scientists and stole his trenchcoat. (All the scientists in this lab wear trenchcoats for some reason.) But you need to make a hasty escape before the unconscious, trenchless scientist is discovered by their peers. 

To play this game, follow this core loop: 

* The Head describes what they see. The game starts in the science lab, so the first description should follow from that. 
* The Hands say, "AND..." and adds an ESCALATION to the description offered by the Head but one that does not CONTRADICT the Head. If the Hands would rather make the Head speak, they can do that by pulling on their tail. The Hands should give a general description of what to say, but the Head is the one who controls speech, so they get to choose the actual words that are spoken. They can also punch. 
* The Feet describe how they move the three critters in a trenchcoat based on the description offered by the Head and Hands. If the Feet do not want to move, they can pull the tail of the Hands critter to make them interact, and they must state that interaction. The Hands are obligated to do what the Feet say. The feet can also dance (to get a bonus maybe, that goes to the hands?) and kick (a door, a cat, or a scientist who is in the way).

For example: 

* The Head says, "I see an open door that leads into the hallway. We should get out of here!"
* The Hands say, "And I hear an alarm sounding! Yikes!"
* The Feet say, "I run out the room and down the hall!"

Next turn: 

* The Head says, "I see a long hallway that looks empty in both directions."
* The Hands say, "And we have no idea where we are!"
* The Feet say, "I pick a direction and run like the dickens!"

Next turn: 

* The Head says, "Two orderlies bust through the doors ahead."
* The Hands say, "And "
