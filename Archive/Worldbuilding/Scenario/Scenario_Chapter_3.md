# Chapter 3: Warehouse 11

Read the following to the players: 

*You make your way down the winding curves of Highway 250 to the Skags' Warehouse District, a.k.a. Churchville. Massive storage buildings stand like black cathedrals that block your view, but you can hear the Staunton Pumps in the distance, noisily working to keep the ever-rising tide at bay.*

*Your nav system finds Warehouse 11 easily enough, and as you pull up, you see its number emblazoned on the outside of the building. A patchwork of drones and security cameras keep watchful eye over this district, and their obviousness seems to drive home the point. You are being watched.*

*You come to realize that Warehouse 11 takes up an entire city block, all the way around. It is three stories tall and windowless. You notice what appears to be a single door on the front of the building, as well as another in the back, along with a large loading dock capable of supporting three semitrailers at once. Every door is accompanied by a bright light and a security camera, both of which are mounted high above.*

## 3A: Assessing the Options

The players may want to discuss their options before making a move on Warehouse 11. There are several different elements of the building's perimeter to consider, as described below. 

### At a Glance

The table below shows the hit points and \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Security|Security]]\] rating of each element of the building's perimeter that the players may want to hack or shoot. (Note that none of these devices has armor.)

| Device                | Hit Points | Security Rating |
| --------------------- | ---------- | --------------- |
| Security Camera       | 5 HP       | Hard (20)       |
| Flood Lights          | 5 HP       | Moderate (14)   |
| Electronic Door Locks | -          | Hard (20)       |
| Drone                 | 10 HP      | Moderate (14)   |

### Security Cameras \[Hacking\]

The building's outer security cameras not only record what they see and hear, they also send notifications to the security guards, along with short video clips, whenever anyone enters or exits the building. They also notify the guards if it appears someone is trying to break in.

The outside, perimeter security cameras have a \[Security\] rating of Hard (20). A security camera can be hacked using the [[Skills_and_Tricks/Hacking/Street_Hack|Street Hack]] trick, but \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Admin|Admin]]\] access is required to disable or otherwise modify the device in any way. At Warehouse 11, all perimeter security cameras belong to the same system. This means that if a player gains \[Admin\] access to one perimeter security camera, they have access to and control over all perimeter cameras.

### Flood Lights \[Hacking\]

A bright, white flood light is mounted on the wall next to each security camera. These lights illuminate the building's doors and loading bays.

Like the security cameras, these outer lights can be compromised with [[Skills_and_Tricks/Hacking/Street_Hack|Street Hack]], and once you have \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Admin|Admin]]\] on one device, you control the whole perimeter light system. The lights have a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Security|Security]]\] rating of Moderate (14).

### Warehouse Doors \[Hacking\]

The front of the building features a single door with a sign that reads, NOT AN ENTRANCE. However, it is, in fact, an entrance. 

At the rear of the building, there is another door, as well as three, large shipping bay doors, the kind that roll up and down when opened and closed. 

All warehouse doors use electronic locks with a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Security|Security]]\] rating of Hard (20). They can be disabled using [[Skills_and_Tricks/Hacking/Disable_Device|Disable Device]] or [[Skills_and_Tricks/Hacking/Street_Hack|Street Hack]]. As with the security cameras and lights, if you gain \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Admin|Admin]]\] access to one perimeter door, you can control them all.

### Drones \[Hacking\]

Warehouse 11 uses automated drones to supplement its on-site security guards. The players can observe a single drone that seems to follow a predictable path as it flies around the building near the top of the third floor. 

The drone is a [[Gear_and_Augments/Drones/Recon_Drones/Gauss_Firefly|Gauss Firefly]]. It can be hacked using [[Skills_and_Tricks/Hacking/Street_Hack|Street Hack]] and has a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Security|Security]]\] rating of Moderate (14). Unlike the other elements of the perimeter, gaining \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Admin|Admin]]\] access to the drone does not translate into access to any other drones. 

### Detected Outside: Security Response \[Combat\]

If the security cameras or drone observe the players as they try to break in to the building, the security guards inside will notified and will respond. Two of the guards will partner up and exit the building from whatever side the players are *not* trying to infiltrate. That is, if the players are breaking in through the front, the guards will exit out the back, or vice versa. They will hustle around the building and attempt to engage the intruders before they can gain entrance to the warehouse.

## 3C. Detected Inside: Security Response \[Combat\]

If the players are detected by the security cameras or the drone as they investigate the warehouse, two security guards will respond. All three guards, along with Malcom Fierro, are holed up in the room at the top of the warehouse. Two of them will partner up and head out of the room and down the metallic stairs, their heavy boots clanging loudly as they go. Their priority is to eliminate the intruders (the players).

## 3D. Rinse and Flush (Optional)

To add to the strangeness of Warehouse 11, you can describe the process of a *rinse and flush*, which is performed on every pod once per month. To include this, interrupt one of the players' turns and read the following: 

*With a metallic whir, the pod nearby lights up on the inside, illuminating its occupant, an elderly man with long, scraggly gray hair and horrid growths on his face and neck. As you look on in disbelief, you see fluid, perhaps water, pour into the pod from nozzles on the inside,*

*The water fills the pod quickly, and just when you think it's going to drown the old man, it stops, just shy of the nostrils. The process pauses long enough for you to wonder if it's finished, but then the water starts to bubble, as if being injected with carbonation. Then, two small flaps open at the base of pod, and you watch as the water drains out onto the concrete floor at your feet.*

*You hear an audible 'ding!' and the light inside the pod turns off.*

### Call Charles Unger

No roll is needed to talk to Charles. The players need only look at the console. When the players call him, read the following:

*Charles answers the phone with wariness and a little trepidation in his voice.* 

*"H- Hello?" He pauses, and then says in a firm, but downbeat voice. "Please tell me Lydia is safe."*

Charles is willing to answer any questions the players might have, but he is most concerned with Lydia's safety. He demands the players share information about her if they know it. 

The players can tell him the truth, or they can choose to lie; it's up to them as to how they handle this situation. However, the fact that Lydia has not logged on since the night before his PONR date makes Charles fear the worst. He cannot make sense of her absence unless she is physically *unable* to logon. 

If the players tell Charles that Lydia is dead, read the following: 

*Charles cries. "No.... I- I can't- Oh my god." He sobs uncontrollably, his sorrow occasionally taking on an unnatural, digital sound as packets drop and the call experiences lag.*

## Aftermath

Now that the players have found Charles Unger, there is just one task left to complete. They need to contact Sigil and report their mission results to her. 

She is disappointed to learn that Charles could not be recovered, but she still compliments them on a job well done. She tells them they should each find 5.000 ⌆ in their accounts. 

*Until next time*, she says. *Keep your noses clean.*
