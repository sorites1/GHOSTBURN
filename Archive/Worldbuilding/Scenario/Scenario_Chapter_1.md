# Chapter 1: Sunshine Apartment Complex

Read the following to the players: 

*You and the rest of your crew are standing in front of the Sunshine Apartment Complex, a no-frills living space on the Panza Lower Side. A cold rain steadily beats down on you as you observe the glass doors at the entrance to the building.*

*Your medium, a woman who goes by Sigil, sent you here to find a guy named Charles Unger. Having a medium in your life has been an adjustment. She's not exactly your boss, but you do owe her, as do the others in your crew. She helped you out after you got burned, and for that, you agreed to do a little, light work on the side.*

*Charles Unger. Disappeared two and a half years ago. Sigil told you the family paid for an investigation, but it came up empty. Now, they have reason to believe he's living here in the Sunshine building. Apartment 416D--fourth floor. That's what you know. 'Bring him back alive,' she said. 'This is a mission of mercy,' whatever that means.*

## 1B. Backdoor

If the players decide to look for a different way in to the Sunshine Apartment Complex, they find a service entrance in the alleyway that runs alongside the building. It has both a pedestrian entrance and a large loading bay door meant for delivery trucks. 

There are no people in the alley. The doors have no windows. A rusted sign reads, *Authorized Personnel Only*.

A security camera is mounted on the wall, about fifteen feet above the door. It blinks silently. If the players try to open either door without first disabling the security camera, an alert is sent to Ranco Security. A squaddie with two patrol officers will arrive in 10 minutes to investigate. The security camera has a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Security|Security]]\] rating of Hard (20).

The service entrance leads to a storage area that gives way to the elevators and stairs, though a valid key is still required.

## 1C. Going Up

There are two ways to get to the fourth floor: the elevator or the stairs.

### Elevators

There are four elevators in the Sunshine Apartment Complex, each of which takes you to a different block: A, B, C, or D. The address given to the players by their medium is 416D, so they should take Elevator D to the fourth floor, which also happens to be the top floor. 

### Stairs

Two stairwells serve as a secondary way of ascending or descending through the building. All blocks (A, B, C, and D) are accessible by both stairwells.

#### NPC Health Levels

An NPC has three health levels, which correspond to their **Health Array**: Uninjured/Hurt/Defeated. The effects of each health level are described below.

| NPC Health Level | NPC Hit Points     | Penalty                     | Description                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ---------------- | ------------------ | --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Uninjured        | More than 50% HP   | None                        | NPC has full hit points. No penalties.                                                                                                                                                            |
| Hurt             | At or Below 50% HP | Their DLs are raised by one | NPC has half (or less) of their hit points remaining. DLs are raised by one.                                                                                                                      |
| Defeated         | At or Below 10% HP | They cannot make rolls      | NPC has 10% (or less) of their hit points remaining. NPC is dead or nearly so. They cannot make rolls, though they may be able to take actions like crawling or speaking, at the GM's discretion. |
