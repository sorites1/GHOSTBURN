# POINT OF NO RETURN

# Introduction

This short scenario is meant to introduce new players and GMs to the rules and world of **GHOSTBURN**. If you are a player, you should **stop reading** now. The material included in this section should only be read by the Game Master (GM).

# Setting

The events of this scenario take place in Ridgeworld, a massive, urban sprawl that blankets the Appalachian Mountains with concrete and steel and which sits on the new coast of the Atlantic Ocean. The players will start in Panza, the middle-class section of the Ridge, where they will find a clue that sends them to a bar in the Skags, the lowest section of the Ridge, in more ways than one.

## Love and Gemstones

Charles Unger was born in Serac at the top of the Ridge to wealthy parents who held influential positions in Knifepoint Capital, the top corporation in the finance industry. As a young man, Charles met and fell in love with a woman named Lydia whose family belonged to Ranco Incorporated. Charles's parents did not approve of the coupling and told their son in no uncertain terms to break off the relationship. They would find him a suitable partner whose family belonged to Knifepoint like them. 

Charles could not bear the thought of cutting Lydia out of his life, so he proposed a plan. The two would escape their corporate cages and flee so they could be together. Before they left, Charles's grandmother paid him a visit. She had somehow learned of his plans despite their secrecy, but she did not scold him or try to coerce him into changing his mind. Instead, she gave him a small, velvet pouch containing a dozen, assorted, uncut gemstones, including emeralds, rubies, and diamonds, along with the documentation transferring ownership of the gems to him. She knew his parents would cut him off financially once he left, and this was her way of showing him love and support. 

## The Disease

Charles and Lydia found an apartment in Lower Panza. Nothing fancy, but it was perfect for them. They lived a simple life, but they were happy because they were together. Six months later, Charles was diagnosed with a horrible disease, one that was not life threatening but which twisted his body and wracked him with pain. According to the doctors, there was no treatment available except a potent sedative that would remove his pain but which would also cloud his mind and leave him unable to engage meaningfully with anyone, including his partner. 

Distraught, Charles and Lydia searched for an unorthodox solution. It was Charles who found the Pod Corporation. The company's pod tech would override his physical senses and replace them with digital ones. He would no longer feel the pain from his body, but it would mean leaving the love of his life. Nevertheless, Lydia supported his idea because there were literally no alternatives they could find. Lydia bought a VR helmet so she could "visit" Charles in the digital world, and they have spent every evening together for the past two years. 

## The Pod Corporation

A **pod person** is someone who has chosen to live their life as a digital construct. To become a pod person, you must have the necessary ports installed in your body, and you have to purchase a lease on a pod. The pod is configured according to your medical needs. It contains a bed and a medical apparatus which is connected to your ports so it can deliver saline, nutrients, and medicine, as needed. Pods are stacked, catalogued, and warehoused so they can be more easily administered by the pod corporation. 

The digital life offered to a pod person can vary according to taste and budget, but most choose a shared experience, much like gamers who join others to play online games. In the virtual world, a pod person is "embodied" by their chosen avatar, and they experience that world through virtual senses (sight, hearing, taste, touch, and smell). They can see, hear, and smell food cooking in the virtual world, but they are oblivious to similar sights, sounds and smells in the real world.

From the day you enter the pod, you get two years before the **point of no return**, the point at which your body is so atrophied and broken that it would be unable to survive outside the pod. For all but the wealthiest customers, no attempt is made to preserve your muscles, nor is any care given to hygiene beyond a basic rinse and flush once per month. It is expected that a pod person will ultimately die in their pod. Luckily, an automated process handles the disposal of expired customers and prepares the pods to be leased again. 

## Digital Will

Before you enter the pod, you must provide the Pod Corporation a copy of your **digital will**. In this document, you are required to list your physical assets and name a beneficiary. Once you hit your point of no return date, your legal status officially changes to that of a **digital entity**, and according to corporate law, digital entities cannot own physical, real-world assets. 

The Pod Corporation is obligated to notify next-of-kin and initiate the transfer of physical property to  beneficiaries in the pod person's digital will. This process does not remove or alter the pod person's control or ownership of digital assets, including their bank accounts (since creds are considered digital assets, not physical ones). 

Charles listed the gemstones he'd received from his grandmother in his digital will, and he wrote Lydia's name as the beneficiary. He wanted to make sure that she would be taken care of, and though he'd not had them appraised, he estimated the gemstones' worth as being six figures, at least. 

He never told her. 

He wanted it to be a surprise and planned to tell her on his PONR date. But that conversation would never take place.

## A Plea for Help

As Charles's point of no return date began to approach, Lydia's mind was occupied with thoughts of financial strain. She was worried she wouldn't be able to continue paying on the pod lease. Worse, she had nowhere to turn, no one to talk to about the problem. She didn't want to burden Charles with it because she felt there was nothing he could to help, not from his pod. And she was not going to ask him to abandon the pod before the date arrived. She knew what misery and pain awaited him if he were to do that. 

So, Lydia swallowed her pride, and did the one thing she never thought she would do. A week before the PONR date, she contacted Charles' parents. She sent a message to them that said that Charles needed their help. She was careful to avoid telling them about his disease or how he'd become a pod person because she didn't want to deliver that news in a simple message. She asked them, humbly, to come to her apartment in the Sunshine Apartment Complex, 416D, and talk with her. She said it was something they should discuss face to face. 

## Sigil, the Medium

When Charles's parents read the message from Lydia, they were incensed. They had no interest in talking to the woman who'd stolen away their son, let alone help her. But they did want their son back. Using their connections, they were put in contact with Sigil, a medium who said she could help them find Charles and bring him home. They gave her the address of the Sunshine Apartment Complex, apartment 416D, and she told them she'd be in touch when the job was done.

## The Scheme

Malcom Fierro works for the Pod Corporation, specifically at Warehouse 11, a facility located in the Skags. Warehouse 11 is configured to support the storage, maintenance, and eventual disposal of *pod people*, customers of Pod Corp. This is where Charles Unger's pod is located.

Malcom is responsible for managing pods that come up coded, PONR, indicating that the occupant has reached their *point of no return*. He is in charge of this notification process meant to inform the Central Authority and next-of-kin, but he hasn't been following the rules. When a pod person in Warehouse 11 gets coded PONR, Malcom reads their digital will to see if any high-value items are on the list. When he sees something juicy, he gives his brother, Ellison "Blister" Fierro, a call. 

## PONR: Charles Unger

When Charles's pod is flagged as PONR, Malcom pulls up Charles's digital will and spots the gemstones straight away. He also notes that the gemstones are being stored at the same address as the beneficiary, a woman named Lydia Martinez. They both reference the Sunshine Apartment Complex, Apt. 416D.

Malcom contacts his brother, Ellison (a.k.a., 'Blister') to tip him off about the gemstones. Blister chooses two soldiers from his gang, "B.B." Bounce Boy and Pixie Stix, and sends them out to locate the gemstones and bring them back. 

The gangers show up at the Sunshine Apartment Complex but are rebuffed by Hector, the front desk clerk. Not wanting to make a scene, they leave and decide to investigate Lydia some more. After a little Net use, the gangers find her picture. They rent a car and set up a stakeout outside her apartment building and when she leaves, they follow her, eventually accosting her. 

Lydia claims to know nothing about the gemstones they are after (she is telling the truth). The gangers coerce her into letting them into her apartment. They return to the Sunshine building, the gangers posing as her invited guests, and go to her apartment. There, the gangers interrogate her further about the gemstones, but she is unable to provide them with any valuable information. Desperate and frustrated, one of the gangers lashes out at Lydia and kills her. 

In a panic, the two gangers start tearing apart the apartment looking for the gemstones. They hear footsteps from the players as they walk down the hall towards 416D and hide before the players arrive in Chapter 1.

## Where *are* the Gemstones?

Charles hid the gemstones in a place that represented his bond with Lydia. He carefully pried open a side panel on her VR helmet, placed the tiny gemstones inside, and then glued the panel back in place. She never discovered them. His plan was to share the news with her on the night of his PONR date, but for the first time in two years, she would not logon. For the first time, Charles was alone.
