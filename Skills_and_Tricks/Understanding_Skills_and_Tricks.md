# Understanding Skills and Tricks

There are fifteen skills in this game, three for each of the core five [[Archive/Abilities_and_Ability_Scores|abilities]] (Presence, Awareness, Agility, Intelligence, and Strength). For example, the three skills associated with Agility are [[Skills_and_Tricks/Drive/Drive_Skill|Drive]], [[Skills_and_Tricks/Firearms/Firearms_Skill|Firearms]], and [[Skills_and_Tricks/Stealth/Stealth_Skill|Stealth]].

If your character knows a skill, they will have a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Skill_Bonus|Skill Bonus]]\] ranging from +1 to +3. When you [[Playing_the_Game/Rolling_the_Dice/Making_a_Roll|Make a Roll]] with a skill, you add your \[Skill Bonus\] and the skill's associated \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Ability_Score|Ability Score]]\] to the roll. 

Each skill has associated \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Trick|Tricks]]\], which are specific uses of the skill. For example, the [[Skills_and_Tricks/Medicine/Medicine_Skill|Medicine]] skill includes a trick called [[Skills_and_Tricks/Medicine/First_Aid|First Aid]] \[Core Trick\] and another called [[Skills_and_Tricks/Medicine/Surgery|Surgery]] \[Signature Trick\].

Each trick is categorized as being either a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Core_Trick|Core Trick]]\] or a \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Signature_Trick|Signature Trick]]\]. You can use any \[Core Trick\] found in these rules, even if you don't have a \[Skill Bonus\] in that skill, but you can only use a \[Signature\] trick if your character knows it.

When you want to try something not covered by these rules, the GM can allow you to perform it as an \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Improvised_Trick|Improvised Trick]]\].
