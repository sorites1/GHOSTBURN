# Reaction

This trick is used in response to a trigger defined in its description. When the condition for the trigger occurs, you may use this trick, even if it's not your turn. \[Reaction\] actions are free (0 AP). 

You have a number of \[Reaction\] actions equal to your Agility score. When you spend a \[Reaction\], it is gone for the rest of the scene. All of your \[Reaction\] actions refresh at the start of a new scene. 

When you use a trick as a \[Reaction\], its outcome is applied before the condition that triggered it. In some cases, this can mean that the triggering condition never actually takes place. 


> [!NOTE] -GET -Reaction //example
> For example, a thug attempts a [[Skills_and_Tricks/Melee/Melee_Strike|Melee Strike]] against you. You spend a \[Reaction\] and roll [[Skills_and_Tricks/Insight/Dodge|Dodge]]. You score a Cool Success, which causes the thug's \[Attack\] to be \[Downgraded\]. The GM rolls for the thug and scores a Success, but due to the player's Dodge, the thug's outcome is \[Downgraded\] to a Fade. This deals \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Minimum_Damage|Minimum Damage]]\], but you are wearing \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Armor|Armor]]\] so the damage is \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Reduced|Reduced]]\]. Because it is already \[Minimum Damage\], it is \[Reduced\] to \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Absolute_Damage|Absolute Damage]]\] of 1. However, Cools Success on [[Skills_and_Tricks/Insight/Dodge|Dodge]] says, "if you would take \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Absolute_Damage|Absolute Damage]]\], you instead take no damage and may immediately \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Move|Move]]\] \[[[Lexicon/Keywords/Keyword_Nearby|Nearby]]\]." In this case, even though the thug rolled a Success, the final result was that no damage was dealt and you got to \[Move\] away for free.
